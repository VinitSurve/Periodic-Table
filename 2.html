Helium

Helium is the second element in the periodic table. It is a colorless, odorless, non-toxic, and inert gas. Helium has the simplest atomic structure, consisting of just two protons and two neutrons. This element is the second most abundant element in the universe, primarily found in stars and gas giant planets. Helium plays a crucial role in various applications, such as cryogenics, balloons, and as a coolant in nuclear reactors.
